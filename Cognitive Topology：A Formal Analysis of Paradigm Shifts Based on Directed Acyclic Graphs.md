
## Abstract
This paper proposes **Cognitive Topology**, a novel framework for the philosophy of science grounded in graph theory. By rigorously modeling human theoretical systems as Directed Acyclic Graphs (DAGs), this study provides a formal classification of theoretical innovations. The research demonstrates that genuinely original ideas correspond to four fundamental graph operations: **genius operations** (source node replacement and hierarchical inversion), **topological stitching** (isomorphic mapping across subgraphs), **unknown unknowns** (introduction of new nodes), and **mediocre operations** (isomorphic expansion). Pseudoscientific practices are identified as noise injection that undermines the legitimacy of graph structures. Combined with case studies from the history of science, this paper reveals the graph-theoretic essence of different innovation types and their contributions to cognitive evolution, and establishes the absolute boundary of human-machine collaboration: artificial intelligence (AI) can generate isomorphic expansions in batches, but cannot evaluate the value of topological stitching or unknown unknowns—these rely on non-computable human intuition.

---
## 1. Theoretical Foundation: DAG as a Formal Model of Cognition
Within the framework of formal science, any theoretical system expressed in natural language can be abstracted as a **Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG)**:
- **Nodes (V)**: Concepts, variables, or states (e.g., socialization, Earth, species). A valid node must be directly or indirectly mapped to an observable event sequence in the physical continuum (latent variables satisfying specific identification conditions are permitted). Vocabulary that cannot complete this mapping (e.g., fate, absolute spirit) is regarded as an inoperable variable in this framework and has no graph-theoretic legitimacy.
- **Directed Edges (E)**: Causal relations or logical deductions (A → B denotes that A causes B or A entails B). A valid edge must be accompanied by dimensional correspondence or a description of dynamic mechanisms. Purely metaphorical lexical adhesion (e.g., the brain is like a computer, society is a machine) without proof of mechanistic isomorphism is deemed an illegal cross-subgraph anomalous edge.
- **Acyclicity**: Acyclicity and temporal unfolding: Logical cycles (A → … → A) within the same time slice are prohibited, as cycles imply circular reasoning or logical deadlock. However, if the author provides an explicit time parameter or state transition equation to unfold a macroscopic cycle into a linear chain on the time axis (e.g., $A_t \to B_{t+1} \to A_{t+2}$), its legitimacy is restored. Dialectical circular arguments that cannot be temporally unfolded are judged as logical deadlocks.

Under this model, academic disputes are no longer lexical debates, but comparisons and operations between graph structures. Theoretical innovation is essentially the editing of DAGs.

## 2. Graph-Theoretic Classification of Originality
Human behavioral patterns when facing an existing theoretical DAG are divided into five types. The first three are legitimate innovations, the fourth is low-value revision, and the fifth is illegal destruction.

### 2.1 Conventional Research: Isomorphic Expansion (P-problem)
- **Operation**: Adding new bypass nodes while recognizing the backbone structure of the original DAG. For example, inserting luck as an intermediary between effort and success.
- **Graph-theoretic Property**: Topological order remains unchanged, the direction of causal arrows is unbroken, and only the number of nodes increases.
- **Essence**: Adding details to an existing map without altering the coordinate origin. Such operations can be easily enumerated by AI. This operation does not constitute a reason for rejection, but only an objective grading standard.

### 2.2 Pseudoscience: Noise Injection (Illegal Operation)
- **Operation**: Ignoring semantic boundaries between nodes to forcibly establish false cross-dimensional connections or introduce directed cycles. For example, quantum entanglement → feng shui layout (without mathematical isomorphism, dimensional correspondence, or testable mechanisms, relying solely on metaphorical adhesion at the lexical level).
- **Graph-theoretic Property**: A judgment of illegality is triggered if any of the following red lines are violated: (a) destroying acyclicity and refusing temporal unfolding; (b) cross-subgraph edges lacking dimensional correspondence or topological isomorphic support; (c) introducing inoperable variables that cannot be mapped to observable event sequences as causal anchors.
- **Essence**: Generating structural noise that cannot be effectively deduced, which has no legitimate graph-theoretic value, elevates the model loss function, causes semantic gradient dissipation, and ultimately prevents the entire reasoning path from converging to a stable attractor.

### 2.3 Genius Operations: Non-isomorphic Reconstruction (NP-hard Problem)
Genius operations alter the topological structure of DAGs and are divided into two non-mutually exclusive subtypes (which often occur in combination):
- **Source Node Replacement**: Replacing a source node with zero in-degree with a new node. The new graph is non-isomorphic to the original one, with shorter path lengths and fewer nodes. Cases: Copernicus (Earth → Sun), Darwin (divine design → variation + selection).
- **Hierarchical Inversion**: Elevating a node at depth k to depth 0 and reversing the direction of related edges, turning the original effect into a cause. Case: Einstein (gravity demoted to an effect of spacetime curvature).

**Note**: Major paradigm revolutions often involve both source node replacement and hierarchical inversion—when a deep node is elevated to the root, the original root node is deleted or demoted.

- **Graph-theoretic Property**: The connectivity of the graph undergoes a qualitative change, and the direction of causal arrows may be reversed. The computational structure of the original graph is reconstructed, and the complexity class of the original reasoning path may change, requiring redefinition in a more powerful representation model.
- **Essence**: A dimensional leap from observational phenomena to underlying reality.

### 2.4 Topological Stitching: Isomorphic Mapping Across Subgraphs
- **Operation**: Discovering mathematical isomorphism between two or more originally disconnected DAG subgraphs and establishing isomorphic mapping edges to stitch them together.
- **Graph-theoretic Property**: Two subgraphs that originally required separate reasoning (total path length $d_1 + d_2$) are compressed to reasoning in only one subgraph (path length $\max(d_1, d_2)$). The internal structure of subgraphs remains unchanged, but hidden equivalence is revealed.
- **Essence**: Merging multiple independent sets of rules into one, significantly reducing learning costs.
- **Typical Cases**: Maxwell mapped Faraday’s lines of force to streamlines in fluid mechanics and discovered the isomorphism between electromagnetic field equations and fluid mechanics equations. Newton’s unification of terrestrial and celestial mechanics also belongs to topological stitching.
- **Corollary**: Isomorphic mapping established by stitching may reveal "vacant nodes" in a subgraph, thereby predicting the existence of unknown entities (e.g., the Dirac equation predicting antimatter, Mendeleev’s periodic table predicting unknown elements).
- **Distinction from Pseudoscientific Noise**: Cross-subgraph connections in topological stitching have strict dimensional correspondence and dynamic mechanism isomorphism; pseudoscientific noise does not.

### 2.5 Unknown Unknowns: Introduction of New Nodes
- **Operation**: Introducing a new node that does not exist in the DAG. The node may temporarily have no outgoing or incoming edges (isolated node), but it opens up a new conceptual dimension.
- **Graph-theoretic Property**: Temporarily does not change graph connectivity, but provides "building materials" for future genius operations or topological stitching.
- **Essence**: Expanding the representation space rather than compressing existing paths. Its value can only be fully realized when edges are supplemented in subsequent research.
- **Typical Cases**: Mendel proposed the gene as a discrete unit of heredity, which was isolated at the time and later became the root node of the entire genetics. Lavoisier introduced the oxygen node to replace phlogiston.
- **Key Constraint**: New nodes must have potential falsifiable paths (even if technically infeasible at present, experiments can be designed for testing in principle). Nodes that cannot generate any falsifiable propositions are demoted to "metaphysical nodes" and classified as pseudoscientific operations.
- **Impact on Loss Function**: Medium-term reduction. New nodes do not immediately reduce the loss function, but once edges are supplemented subsequently, the reasoning cost of related tasks plummets.

### 2.6 Metaphysical Pioneers: Expansion of the Cognitive Frontier
- **Definition**: Introducing new nodes or frameworks that explicitly do not claim empirical falsifiability, but are self-consistent, explanatory, or heuristic at the conceptual level, expanding the space of human cognitive possibilities.
- **Distinction from Pseudoscientific Noise**:
  - Pseudoscience: Disguises itself as science, constructs cross-subgraph anomalous edges using scientific terminology, and refuses empirical testing.
  - Pioneers: Explicitly do not disguise themselves as science, use their own terminology system, and do not require empirical testing. Their legitimacy depends on conceptual self-consistency and cognitive expansion rather than falsifiability.
- **Distinction from Unknown Unknowns**:
  - Unknown Unknowns: New nodes have potential falsifiable paths, and their value partially depends on future empirical verification (e.g., Mendel’s genes).
  - Pioneers: New nodes are unfalsifiable in principle, and their value lies in opening new dimensions for thinking rather than future empirical verification.
- **Judgment Criteria**:
  1. The text explicitly states that it is outside the scope of empirical science, or its non-empirical scientific attribute can be clearly inferred from the context.
  2. The conceptual structure is self-consistent without vicious circularity (directed cycles for self-referential definition shall be evaluated for logical halting risks).
  3. It has an identifiable expansion effect on the cognitive space (providing new conceptual tools, distinctions, or perspectives).
- **Typical Cases**:
  - Plato’s Theory of Forms: Introducing the "world of Forms" as the foundation of the sensible world, unfalsifiable but shaping the problem consciousness of Western philosophy for two thousand years.
  - Kant’s Thing-in-Itself: Postulating an unknowable reality to demarcate the boundaries of reason, unfalsifiable but reconstructing the coordinate system of epistemology.
  - Laozi’s Dao: As an ineffable ultimate reality, unfalsifiable but providing a core metaphysical anchor for Chinese thought.
- **Graph-theoretic Property**: Introducing one or more new nodes into the cognitive DAG, which may have incoming edges (derived from empirical concepts) but no outgoing edges for testable predictions in principle. They do not compress existing paths but expand the node set of the graph, reserving potential anchors for subsequent topological stitching or genius operations. Their value lies in increasing the dimensionality of the representation space rather than reducing the reasoning cost of existing paths.
- **Impact on Loss Function**: No direct reduction. The introduction of pioneer nodes does not immediately improve AI’s prediction accuracy on empirical tasks. However, if the node is supplemented with testable edges by subsequent thinkers (i.e., evolving from a pioneer to an unknown unknown) or becomes a candidate root node for genius operations, its long-term cognitive value is realized.

## 3. Graph-Theoretic Mechanisms of Scientific Revolutions
### Law 1: Topological Folding (Path Compression)
Genius operations compress long paths forcibly extended to fit anomalies in the original DAG into the intrinsic properties of a single node through source node replacement or hierarchical inversion.
- Copernicus: Over 70 epicycle nodes → the single node of Earth’s revolution.
- Darwin: Countless independent species nodes → the recursive process of variation + selection.

### Law 2: Source Node Anchoring and Legitimacy Declaration
Source nodes in empirical science must be anchored to physical reality or observable event sequences. If a source node is a macroscopic emergent property (e.g., utility, institution) or a pure mathematical structure (e.g., state vectors in Hilbert space), the author must explicitly declare its anchoring type (empirical anchoring/emergent anchoring/mathematical self-consistent anchoring) in the introduction or methodology. Source nodes that are undeclared and untraceable to the physical continuum are judged as suspended nodes.

### Law 3: Isomorphic Compression (A Special Case of Topological Stitching)
When subgraphs in two different fields are proven isomorphic, the learning cost is reduced from mastering two sets of rules to one. For example, Maxwell unified electromagnetism and fluid mechanics.

### Law 4: Node Preparation (A Special Case of Unknown Unknowns)
Introducing a temporarily isolated new node is equivalent to reserving a "candidate root node" for future theories. Its value is realized when edges are supplemented subsequently.

## 4. Formal Reconstruction of the History of Ideas: Nine Cases
The following presents the graph-theoretic operations corresponding to major theoretical transformations in chronological order.

### Case 1: Aristotle → Newton (Genius Operation: Source Node Replacement)
- Original Graph: Source node natural teleology, unmeasurable, requiring a large number of auxiliary nodes to explain projectiles.
- Operation: Introducing the new source node force ($F=ma$) and cutting outgoing edges of the original source node.
- Advantage of the New Graph: The source node is anchored to measurable physical quantities, and the graph diameter is greatly shortened.

### Case 2: Ptolemy → Copernicus (Genius Operation: Source Node Replacement + Path Compression)
- Original Graph: Source node Earth, continuously adding epicycle nodes to fit retrograde motion, with $O(N)$ nodes.
- Operation: Replacing the source node with the Sun and compressing long paths into the attribute of Earth’s revolution.
- Advantage of the New Graph: Constant number of nodes, predicting the phases of Venus.

### Case 3: Newton → Einstein (Genius Operation: Source Node Demotion + Hierarchical Inversion)
- Original Graph: Source node gravity, unable to fit the precession of Mercury’s perihelion.
- Operation: Introducing the new source node spacetime metric and demoting gravity to a derived node.
- Advantage of the New Graph: Predicting the precession of Mercury’s perihelion without additional nodes, and reducing to the Newtonian graph in the weak-field approximation.

### Case 4: Fixism of Species → Darwin (Genius Operation: Cycle Excision + Source Node Replacement)
- Original Graph: Source node divine design, implying directed cycles and being unfalsifiable.
- Operation: Excising cycles and replacing the source node with random variation + environmental selection.
- Advantage of the New Graph: Acyclic, extremely short graph diameter, and falsifiable.

### Case 5: Blending Inheritance → Mendel (Unknown Unknowns: New Node Introduction)
- Original Graph: Parental traits → offspring traits (blending), with discrete traits as isolated nodes.
- Operation: Deprecating the old hypothesis, introducing the new node gene, and establishing a genotype-phenotype mapping.
- Advantage of the New Graph: Isolated phenomena obtain causal paths, predicting the 3:1 segregation ratio. The subsequent discovery of the DNA double helix supplemented edges, making gene the root node.

### Case 6: Electromagnetism → Maxwell’s Equations (Topological Stitching: Isomorphic Mapping Across Subgraphs)
- Original Graph: The electromagnetism subgraph and fluid mechanics subgraph were disconnected and independent.
- Operation: Discovering the mathematical isomorphism between electromagnetic field equations and fluid mechanics equations and establishing isomorphic mapping edges.
- Advantage of the New Graph: Research results in fluid mechanics can be directly mapped to electromagnetism, reducing learning costs.

### Case 7: Terrestrial and Celestial Mechanics → Newton (Topological Stitching)
- Original Graph: Galileo’s terrestrial motion and Kepler’s planetary motion were regarded as two independent subgraphs.
- Operation: Newton unified the two with the law of universal gravitation and laws of motion, establishing isomorphic mapping.
- Advantage of the New Graph: A single set of mechanical rules applies to all things on Earth and in the heavens.

### Case 8: Adam Smith, Marx, Neoclassical Economics (Orthogonal Subgraphs, Pending Stitching)
- Three Graphs: Source nodes are self-interest (psychological), labor (social), and utility (formalized psychology), which are orthogonal to each other.
- Diagnosis: Any current attempt to directly establish directed edges between these three root nodes constitutes illegal cross-subgraph connection due to the lack of dimensional correspondence and dynamic isomorphism. Legitimate topological stitching must be reduced downward to find a more underlying isomorphic mechanism (e.g., energy and information flow) as a common subgraph.

### Case 9: Classical Mechanics → Quantum Mechanics (Genius Operation: Source Node Replacement + Path Summation)
- Original Graph: Source node definite position and momentum of particles, deterministic trajectories.
- Operation: Replacing the source node with the initial wave function and adopting path summation.
- Advantage of the New Graph: Eliminating the contradictory node of wave-particle duality, and reducing to the classical graph in the macroscopic limit.

## 5. The Absolute Boundary of Human-Machine Collaboration
1. AI can generate isomorphic expansions in batches and perform rigid structural demining: Given an original graph, AI can enumerate legal addition and reversal operations to generate patchwork innovations. More importantly, AI acts as an enforcer of structural legitimacy: by checking temporal unfolding, dimensional mapping, and observable anchoring, it judges metaphorical adhesion, dialectical deadlocks, and undeclared metaphysics as illegal.
2. AI cannot evaluate the value of non-isomorphic reconstruction and unknown unknowns: Genius operations require assessing the ability to dimensionally absorb old theories, involving physical intuition of causal depth that is non-computable. Unknown unknowns demand predicting the potential explanatory power of new nodes, a sensitivity to gaps that cannot be enumerated by algorithms. AI can discover mathematical isomorphisms in high-dimensional spaces (assisting topological stitching), but cannot judge the ontological significance of such isomorphisms in the physical continuum.

Therefore, humans are and only are the legislators of cognitive topology—they adjudicate which causal connection mode qualifies as "science" and which new node is worthy of future investment.

## 6. Ultimate Conclusion
Through formal abstraction from the DAG perspective, the innovative essence of the history of human ideas is fully revealed:
- Mediocrities add nodes in isomorphic expansion;
- Pseudoscientists damage graph structures in noise injection;
- Geniuses compress paths through source node replacement or hierarchical inversion;
- Stitchers merge rules through isomorphic mapping across subgraphs;
- Pioneers prepare future root nodes by introducing new nodes.

In the era of artificial intelligence, all theoretical structures that can be mass-produced by AI are mediocre; the value anchor of all truly original topological operations always and only lies in non-computable human intuition. Humans are no longer the sole producers of knowledge, but remain the legislators of cognitive topology.

In the court of DAGs, there are no inviolable concepts, only legitimacy and illegitimacy, originality and mediocrity. This is the entirety of Cognitive Topology.

## Addendum: Three Auxiliary Operations—Debridement, Demolition, and Suspension Before Genius Surgery
Genius operations, topological stitching, and unknown unknowns cannot be directly performed on contaminated original graphs. The following three preprocessing steps are necessary but do not constitute original innovation in themselves.

### 1. Feedback Edge Truncation (Demolition)
Excising directed cycles to restore acyclicity. Darwin cut off the circular reasoning of creationism, but this did not equate to proposing the theory of evolution.

### 2. Cross-Subgraph Anomalous Edge Elimination (Clearance)
Deleting illegal cross-subgraph edges and proving that "A cannot entail B", but not providing "C". Hume severed the is-ought gap, but did not establish a new ethical graph.

### 3. Isolated Node Deprecation (Suspension)
Shielding inoperable metaphysical nodes to free up space. Mendel deprecated blending inheritance to make room for the gene node.

These three operations answer "what is wrong with the original graph"; genuine innovation answers "how to construct the new graph". The former is deconstruction, and the latter is construction. In the history of ideas, those who deconstruct without constructing are called "critics"; those who construct without deconstructing are called "dogmatists"; those who deconstruct first and then construct are called "revolutionaries". A true paradigm shift is the continuous completion of deconstruction and construction.

## Note on Quantum Mechanics
Quantum mechanics involves probability amplitudes, superposition states, and measurement collapse, with a causal structure different from classical science. The DAG framework in this paper is mainly applicable to theoretical systems with deterministic causal chains. Therefore, Case 9 only presents structural comparisons and does not claim that DAGs can fully describe quantum mechanics. However, the DAG judgment rules (source node anchoring, acyclicity, prohibition of cross-subgraph edges) can still be used to identify pseudoscientific quantum mechanics: legitimate quantum mechanics anchors source nodes to state vectors in Hilbert space; pseudoscience uses isolated nodes such as consciousness to impersonate source nodes, or links entanglement to telepathy. This paper does not attempt to prove the universality of the framework through quantum mechanics, but the framework is sufficient to perform a fundamental task: eliminating fallacies from genuine science.
