
## Abstract
This paper proposes **Cognitive Topology**, a novel framework for the philosophy of science grounded in graph theory. By rigorously modeling human theoretical systems as Directed Acyclic Graphs (DAGs), the study formalizes the classification of theoretical innovations. It is found that genuinely original ideas correspond to four fundamental graph operations: *genius operations* (source-node replacement and hierarchical inversion), *topological stitching* (isomorphic mapping across subgraphs), *unknown unknowns* (introduction of new nodes), and *mediocrity operations* (isomorphic expansion). Pseudoscientific practices are identified as noise injection that undermines the legitimacy of graph structures. Drawing on case studies from the history of science, this paper reveals the graph-theoretic essence of different innovation types and their contributions to cognitive evolution. It further establishes the absolute boundary of human-machine collaboration: AI can mass-produce isomorphic expansions, yet it cannot evaluate the value of topological stitching or unknown unknowns—these rely on non-computable human intuition.

---

## 1. Theoretical Foundation: DAG as a Formal Model of Cognition
Within the framework of formal science, any theoretical system expressed in natural language can be abstracted into a Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG), defined by three core components:
1.  **Nodes (V)**: Concepts, variables, or states (e.g., "socialization", "Earth", "species").
2.  **Directed Edges (E)**: Causal relationships or logical deductions (A → B denotes that A causes B or A logically entails B).
3.  **Acyclicity**: Prohibition of cyclic paths such as A → … → A. Cycles imply circular reasoning or logical deadlocks, rendering the system undecidable.

Under this model, academic debates are no longer verbal disputes but comparisons and manipulations between graph structures. Theoretical innovation, in essence, is the editing of DAGs.

## 2. Graph-Theoretic Classification of Originality
Human behaviors toward existing theoretical DAGs are categorized into five types. The first three represent legitimate innovations, the fourth denotes low-value patching, and the fifth constitutes illegal disruption.

### 2.1 Mediocrity: Isomorphic Expansion (P Problem)
- **Operation**: Adding new bypass nodes while preserving the backbone structure of the original DAG. For example, inserting "luck" as an intermediary between "effort" and "success".
- **Graph-Theoretic Property**: The topological order remains unchanged; causal edge directions are unbroken; only the number of nodes increases.
- **Essence**: Adding details to an existing cognitive map without shifting the coordinate origin. This task can be easily exhaustively generated by AI.

### 2.2 Pseudoscience: Noise Injection (Illegal Operation)
- **Operation**: Establishing spurious cross-dimensional connections or introducing directed cycles by ignoring semantic boundaries between nodes. For example, claiming "quantum entanglement → feng shui layout" without providing mathematical isomorphism, dimensional correspondence, or testable mechanisms—relying solely on metaphorical word association.
- **Graph-Theoretic Property**: Violating acyclicity, introducing explanatory cycles or abnormal cross-subgraph edges, leading to logical halting.
- **Essence**: Generating structural noise that invalidates effective deduction. Such operations hold no legitimate graph-theoretic value; they elevate model loss functions, cause semantic gradient dissipation, and ultimately prevent reasoning paths from converging to stable attractors.

### 2.3 Genius Operation: Non-Isomorphic Reconstruction (NP-Hard Problem)
Genius operations alter the topological structure of DAGs, comprising two non-mutually exclusive subtypes (often occurring in combination):
1.  **Source-Node Replacement**: Substituting a root node (with in-degree zero) with a new node. The new graph is non-isomorphic to the old one, featuring shorter path lengths and fewer nodes. Case studies: Copernicus (replacing Earth with the Sun as the solar system's center), Darwin (replacing divine design with variation + natural selection).
2.  **Hierarchical Inversion**: Elevating a node at depth k to depth 0 and reversing the direction of associated edges, transforming a "result" into a "cause". Case study: Einstein (relegating gravity to an effect of spacetime curvature).

**Note**: Major paradigm shifts typically involve both source-node replacement and hierarchical inversion—when a deep-layer node is promoted to the root, the original root node is either deleted or demoted.

- **Graph-Theoretic Property**: The connectivity of the graph is altered; causal edge directions may be reversed. The computational structure of the old graph is reconstructed, potentially changing the complexity class of original reasoning paths, which must be redefined within a more powerful representation model.
- **Essence**: A dimensional leap from observational phenomena to underlying reality.

### 2.4 Topological Stitching: Isomorphic Mapping Across Subgraphs
- **Operation**: Identifying mathematical isomorphism between two or more originally disconnected DAG subgraphs and establishing isomorphic mapping edges to stitch them together.
- **Graph-Theoretic Property**: Two subgraphs that previously required separate reasoning (with total path length d₁ + d₂) are compressed into a single subgraph for inference (with path length max(d₁, d₂)). The internal structure of subgraphs remains unchanged, but hidden equivalences are revealed.
- **Essence**: Merging multiple independent rule sets into one, drastically reducing learning costs.
- **Typical Case Studies**: Maxwell mapped Faraday's "lines of force" to the "streamlines" of fluid mechanics, discovering the isomorphism between electromagnetic field equations and fluid dynamics equations. Newton's unification of terrestrial and celestial mechanics also falls under topological stitching.
- **Corollary**: Isomorphic mappings established via stitching may reveal "missing nodes" in a subgraph, enabling the prediction of unknown entities (e.g., Dirac's equation predicting antimatter, Mendeleev's periodic table predicting undiscovered elements).
- **Distinction from Pseudoscientific Noise**: Cross-subgraph connections in topological stitching are supported by strict dimensional correspondence and dynamic mechanism isomorphism; pseudoscientific noise lacks such foundations.

### 2.5 Unknown Unknowns: Introduction of New Nodes
- **Operation**: Introducing a previously non-existent new node into the DAG. This node may initially have no incoming or outgoing edges (an isolated node) but opens up a new conceptual dimension.
- **Graph-Theoretic Property**: Does not temporarily alter the connectivity of the graph, but provides "building materials" for future genius operations or topological stitching.
- **Essence**: Expanding the representation space rather than compressing existing paths. Its value can only be fully realized when subsequent edges are added through follow-up research.
- **Typical Case Studies**: Mendel proposed "genes" as discrete units of heredity—an isolated concept at the time, which later became the root node of genetics. Lavoisier introduced the "oxygen" node to replace "phlogiston".
- **Key Constraint**: A new node must possess potential falsifiable paths (even if currently unfeasible with existing technology, experiments must be designable in principle). If no falsifiable propositions can be derived, it is demoted to a "metaphysical node", qualifying as a pseudoscientific operation.
- **Impact on Loss Function**: Medium-term reduction. The new node itself does not immediately lower the loss function, but once subsequent edges are added, the reasoning cost of related tasks plummets.

### 2.6 Metaphysical Pioneers: Expansion of Cognitive Frontiers
- **Definition**: Introducing new nodes or frameworks that explicitly do not claim empirical falsifiability, yet exhibit conceptual consistency, explanatory power, or heuristic value, expanding the possibility space of human cognition.
- **Distinction from Pseudoscientific Noise**:
  - Pseudoscience: Disguises itself as science, uses scientific terminology to construct abnormal cross-subgraph edges, but rejects empirical testing.
  - Metaphysical Pioneers: Explicitly does not pretend to be science, employs its own terminological system, and does not require empirical testing. Its legitimacy depends on conceptual consistency and cognitive expansion, not falsifiability.
- **Distinction from Unknown Unknowns**:
  - Unknown Unknowns: New nodes have potential falsifiable paths, and their value partially relies on future empirical validation (e.g., Mendel's genes).
  - Metaphysical Pioneers: New nodes are in principle unfalsifiable; their value lies in opening up new dimensions of thought, independent of future empirical evidence.
- **Judgment Criteria**:
  1.  The text explicitly states that it does not belong to the realm of empirical science, or its non-empirical nature can be clearly inferred from the context.
  2.  The conceptual structure is self-consistent, free from vicious cycles (if a directed cycle exists as a self-referential definition, it is necessary to evaluate whether it causes logical halting).
  3.  It has identifiable cognitive expansion effects (providing new conceptual tools, distinctions, or perspectives).
- **Typical Case Studies**:
  - Plato's Theory of Forms: Introducing the "world of Forms" as the foundation of the sensible world—unfalsifiable, yet shaping two thousand years of Western philosophical problem consciousness.
  - Kant's Thing-in-Itself: Positing an unknowable realm of reality, delineating the boundaries of reason—unfalsifiable, yet reconstructing the coordinate system of epistemology.
  - Laozi's Dao: As the ineffable ultimate reality—unfalsifiable, yet providing the core metaphysical anchor for Chinese thought.
- **Graph-Theoretic Property**: Introducing one or more new nodes into the cognitive DAG. These nodes may have incoming edges (derived from empirical concepts) but, in principle, no outgoing edges leading to testable predictions. They do not compress existing paths but expand the node set of the graph, reserving potential anchor points for future topological stitching or genius operations. Their value lies in increasing the dimensionality of the representation space, not reducing the reasoning cost of existing paths.
- **Impact on Loss Function**: No direct reduction. The introduction of pioneer nodes does not immediately improve the prediction accuracy of AI in empirical tasks. However, if these nodes are supplemented with testable edges by subsequent thinkers (evolving from pioneers to unknown unknowns) or become root node candidates for genius operations, their long-term cognitive value is realized.

## 3. Graph-Theoretic Mechanisms of Scientific Revolutions
### Law 1: Topological Folding (Path Compression)
Genius operations compress long paths in the old DAG—previously extended to accommodate anomalies—into intrinsic properties of a single node via source-node replacement or hierarchical inversion.
- Copernicus: Reducing over seventy epicycle nodes to the single node of "Earth's revolution".
- Darwin: Reducing countless independent species nodes to the recursive process of "variation + selection".

### Law 2: Empirical Anchoring
The new root node must correspond to physical reality, not observational phenomena. The old graph anchors to phenomena (Ptolemy anchored to the "terrestrial observation point"; creationism anchored to the "perfect form of species"); the new graph anchors to reality (Copernicus anchored to the "solar gravitational center"; Darwin anchored to "genetic variation and survival pressure").

### Law 3: Isomorphic Compression (A Special Case of Topological Stitching)
When two subgraphs from different fields are proven isomorphic, the learning cost decreases from mastering two sets of rules to one. For example, Maxwell's unification of electromagnetism and fluid mechanics.

### Law 4: Node Preparation (A Special Case of Unknown Unknowns)
Introducing a temporarily isolated new node is equivalent to reserving a "root node candidate" for future theories. Its value is realized when subsequent edges are added.

## 4. Formal Reconstruction of Intellectual History: Nine Case Studies
The following presents the graph-theoretic operations corresponding to major theoretical transformations in chronological order.

### Case 1: Aristotle → Newton (Genius Operation: Source-Node Replacement)
- **Old Graph**: Root node of "natural purpose"—unmeasurable, requiring numerous auxiliary nodes to explain projectile motion.
- **Operation**: Introducing the new root node of "force" (F=ma) and cutting off the outgoing edges of the old root node.
- **Advantages of the New Graph**: The root node anchors to measurable physical quantities, drastically shortening the graph diameter.

### Case 2: Ptolemy → Copernicus (Genius Operation: Source-Node Replacement + Path Compression)
- **Old Graph**: Root node of "Earth"—requiring a continuous increase in epicycle nodes to fit retrograde motion, with the number of nodes scaling as O(N).
- **Operation**: Replacing the root node with the "Sun" and compressing long paths into the property of "Earth's revolution".
- **Advantages of the New Graph**: The number of nodes becomes constant, enabling the prediction of Venus' phases.

### Case 3: Newton → Einstein (Genius Operation: Source-Node Demotion + Hierarchical Inversion)
- **Old Graph**: Root node of "gravity"—unable to fit the perihelion precession of Mercury.
- **Operation**: Introducing the new root node of "spacetime metric" and demoting "gravity" to a derived node.
- **Advantages of the New Graph**: Predicting Mercury's perihelion precession without additional nodes; degenerating into Newton's graph under weak-field approximation.

### Case 4: Fixism → Darwin (Genius Operation: Cycle Excision + Source-Node Replacement)
- **Old Graph**: Root node of "divine design"—implicitly containing directed cycles, unfalsifiable.
- **Operation**: Excising cycles and replacing the root node with "random variation + environmental selection".
- **Advantages of the New Graph**: Acyclic structure with an extremely short diameter, falsifiable.

### Case 5: Blending Inheritance → Mendel (Unknown Unknowns: Introduction of New Nodes)
- **Old Graph**: Parental traits → offspring traits (blending), with discrete traits existing as isolated nodes.
- **Operation**: Setting aside the old hypothesis and introducing the new node of "gene", establishing the genotype → phenotype mapping.
- **Advantages of the New Graph**: Isolated phenomena acquire causal paths, enabling the prediction of the 3:1 segregation ratio. Subsequent supplementation of edges via the DNA double helix model elevated "gene" to a root node.

### Case 6: Electromagnetism → Maxwell's Equations (Topological Stitching: Isomorphic Mapping Across Subgraphs)
- **Old Graph**: The electromagnetism subgraph and fluid mechanics subgraph were disconnected and independent.
- **Operation**: Discovering the mathematical isomorphism between electromagnetic field equations and fluid dynamics equations, establishing isomorphic mapping edges.
- **Advantages of the New Graph**: Research achievements in fluid mechanics can be directly mapped to electromagnetism, reducing learning costs.

### Case 7: Terrestrial Mechanics & Celestial Mechanics → Newton (Topological Stitching)
- **Old Graph**: Galileo's terrestrial object motion and Kepler's planetary motion were regarded as two independent subgraphs.
- **Operation**: Newton unified the two via the law of universal gravitation and laws of motion, establishing isomorphic mappings.
- **Advantages of the New Graph**: A single set of mechanical rules applies to all objects in heaven and on Earth.

### Case 8: Adam Smith, Marx, Neoclassical Economics (Orthogonal Subgraphs Pending Stitching)
- **Three Graphs**: Root nodes of "self-interest" (psychological), "labor" (social), and "utility" (psychological formalization), respectively—mutually orthogonal.
- **Diagnosis**: Forced edge connection is illegal; topological stitching requires identifying a more fundamental root node (e.g., energy and information flow), which remains unaccomplished to date.

### Case 9: Classical Mechanics → Quantum Mechanics (Genius Operation: Source-Node Replacement + Path Integral)
- **Old Graph**: Root node of "definite particle position and momentum"—deterministic trajectories.
- **Operation**: Replacing the root node with the "initial wave function" and implementing path integration.
- **Advantages of the New Graph**: Eliminating the contradictory node of "wave-particle duality"; degenerating into the classical graph under macroscopic limits.

## 5. Absolute Boundaries of Human-Machine Collaboration
1.  **AI can mass-produce isomorphic expansions**: Given an old graph G<sub>old</sub>, AI can traverse all legal addition and inversion operations to generate a large number of mediocre "patchwork innovations".
2.  **AI cannot evaluate the value of non-isomorphic reconstruction, topological stitching, or unknown unknowns**:
    - Genius operations require assessing the ability to "subsume old theories via dimensional reduction" and "generate falsifiable propositions", involving causal depth judgment which is non-computable.
    - Topological stitching requires identifying cross-domain isomorphism, relying on intuition for abstract structures.
    - Unknown unknowns require predicting the potential explanatory power of a new node—this "sensitivity to blank spaces" cannot be exhaustively enumerated by algorithms.

Thus, humans are and will remain the sole legislators of cognitive topology—they adjudicate which causal connections qualify as "science" and which new nodes are worthy of future investment.

---

## 6. Ultimate Conclusions
Through formal dissection from a DAG perspective, the essential nature of innovation in human intellectual history is laid bare:
- Mediocrities add nodes via isomorphic expansion;
- Pseudoscientists destroy graph structures via noise injection;
- Geniuses compress paths via source-node replacement or hierarchical inversion;
- Stitchers merge rules via cross-subgraph isomorphic mapping;
- Pioneers prepare future root nodes via new node introduction.

In the age of artificial intelligence, any theoretical structure that can be mass-produced by AI is mediocre; the value anchor of genuinely original topological operations will forever reside in non-computable human intuition. Humans are no longer the sole producers of knowledge, but they remain the legislators of cognitive topology.

In the court of DAGs, no concept is sacred and inviolable—only legality vs. illegality, originality vs. mediocrity matter. This is the entirety of Cognitive Topology.

## Supplementary Discussion: Three Auxiliary Operations—Debridement, Wall Demolition, and Suspension Before Genius Operations
Genius operations, topological stitching, and unknown unknowns cannot be directly executed on contaminated old graphs. Three preprocessing steps are necessary, though they do not constitute original innovation in themselves:

### 1. Feedback Edge Truncation (Wall Demolition)
Excising directed cycles to restore acyclicity. Darwin excised the circular reasoning of creationism, but this did not equate to proposing the theory of evolution.

### 2. Cross-Subgraph Abnormal Edge Elimination (Debridement)
Deleting illegal cross-subgraph edges and proving that "A cannot entail B", without providing an alternative "C". Hume severed the "is → ought" connection, but did not establish a new ethical graph.

### 3. Isolated Node Cold Storage (Suspension)
Shielding inoperable metaphysical nodes to free up cognitive space. Mendel set aside "blending inheritance" to make room for the "gene" node.

These three operations answer the question: *Where is the old graph wrong?* Genuine innovation answers the question: *How should the new graph be constructed?* The former is destruction; the latter is construction. In intellectual history:
- Those who destroy without constructing are called "critics";
- Those who construct without destroying are called "dogmatists";
- Those who destroy first and then construct are called "revolutionaries".
A true paradigm shift is the continuous completion of both destruction and construction.

## Note on Quantum Mechanics
Quantum mechanics involves probability amplitudes, superposition states, and measurement collapse, whose causal structure differs from that of classical science. The DAG framework in this paper is primarily applicable to theoretical systems with deterministic causal chains. Thus, Case 9 only presents a structural comparison and does not claim that DAGs can fully describe quantum mechanics. Nevertheless, the DAG judgment rules (source-node anchoring, acyclicity, prohibition of cross-subgraph edges) remain valid for distinguishing pseudoscientific quantum mechanics:
- Legitimate quantum mechanics anchors its root nodes to state vectors in Hilbert space;
- Pseudoscience uses isolated nodes such as "consciousness" as pseudo-root nodes, or connects "entanglement" to "telepathy".

This paper does not attempt to prove the universality of the framework via quantum mechanics. Instead, the framework is sufficient to perform one fundamental task: separating nonsense from genuine science.
